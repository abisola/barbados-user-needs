Chat-based government service delivery — platform stack

1
Canonical registers & reference data
Foundation
▶
What's needed

Authoritative, curated, machine-readable lists that act as single sources of truth — managed as first-class products with named custodians, version control, and APIs.

Key registers
Government offices & service centres Addresses & locations Government services (canonical list) Government organisations Fees & charges Legislation & regulations index Parishes & constituencies Forms & documents register Holidays & civic dates
What likely exists today

Scattered spreadsheets, individual ministry websites, civil servant knowledge. Little machine-readable or API-accessible. OpenCRVS may establish some patterns for civil registration.

Gap: Register framework & standard · Starter set audit (orgs, offices, services) · Named custodians per register · Publication mechanism (CSV/JSON → API) · Change governance process
2
Structured content & knowledge base
Foundation
▶
What's needed

A curated, authoritative, up-to-date corpus of government service information — the single most important prerequisite. Without good content, even the best AI produces poor answers.

Includes
Plain-language service descriptions Eligibility criteria as structured data Forms & requirements Fee schedules Contact details & opening hours Legislation & policy summaries FAQs from actual citizen enquiries
What likely exists today

Some content on ministry websites — fragmented, inconsistently structured, often out of date. alpha.gov.bb has begun establishing patterns but coverage is limited.

Gap: Content audit across priority services · Structured content model & schema · Content operations process · Ministry content owners · Central CMS as single source of truth
3
Retrieval & AI layer ("the brain")
Intelligence
▶
What's needed

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) architecture: combines an LLM with a retrieval system that fetches relevant content before generating a response. Grounded in the knowledge base.

Key components
LLM (hosted API or self-hosted) Vector database Embedding pipeline Orchestration layer Guardrails & safety systems Human-in-the-loop escalation
What likely exists today

Essentially nothing purpose-built. Some ad hoc LLM experimentation possible but no production-grade RAG infrastructure.

Gap: LLM strategy (hosted vs self-hosted, data sovereignty) · Vector DB + embedding pipeline · Orchestration with safety guardrails · Prompt engineering for Barbadian context · Evaluation framework
4
Identity & authentication
Trust
▶
What's needed

Tiered identity assurance to enable the chatbot to move beyond information-giving into transactional services with appropriate verification at each risk level.

Tiers
Anonymous — general info, Phase 1 Light verification — status checks Authenticated — full transactions Consent management
What likely exists today

No national digital identity system in production. Individual ministry systems may have siloed logins.

Gap: Identity assurance framework with tiered levels · Phase 1 focus on anonymous tier (no infra needed) · Plan for integration with future national digital identity · Data retention policies for conversation data
5
Integration & interoperability layer
Plumbing
▶
What's needed

Connections to back-end government systems for anything beyond general Q&A — case management, licensing, registration, payments. The most expensive and time-consuming layer.

Key components
API gateway APIs on existing systems Data exchange layer (X-Road model) Integration bus / middleware
What likely exists today

Most systems likely siloed with little or no API exposure. Some newer systems (formsflow.ai etc.) may have APIs; legacy systems almost certainly don't.

Gap: Audit of priority systems & integration readiness · API gateway deployment · API wrappers/adapters for priority systems · Data exchange standards · Sequence by highest-value integrations first
6
Channels & front-end
Interface
▶
What's needed

Citizen-facing interfaces — separate from the AI "brain" so the same back-end can serve multiple channels simultaneously.

Key channels
Web chat widget (alpha.gov.bb) WhatsApp (high Caribbean penetration) SMS (inclusion fallback) Voice (phone-based) In-person kiosk
What likely exists today

alpha.gov.bb website. Possibly some ministry social media. No unified conversational channel.

Gap: Web chat on alpha.gov.bb as first channel · WhatsApp Business API access & cost assessment · Channel-agnostic architecture · SMS fallback for inclusion · User research on channel preferences
7
Payments
Transactions
▶
What's needed

Payment integration for services involving fees — licence renewals, permits, fines — when the chat interface moves to transactional services. Not a day-one requirement.

Key components
Payment gateway (Barbados cards) Powertranz / FAC / BiMPay / EZPay Payment links in conversation Reconciliation & receipt generation PCI DSS compliance (hosted pages)
What likely exists today

Some government payment infrastructure exists (Powertranz/FAC in some contexts) but landscape is fragmented.

Gap: Not a Phase 1 priority · Simplest approach: payment links → existing hosted pages · Align with GovTech payment infrastructure decisions · Longer term: inline payment within chat
8
Analytics, monitoring & continuous improvement
Operations
▶
What's needed

Without robust analytics you're flying blind. The chat interface generates rich data on citizen needs, pain points, and service gaps — but only if instrumented from day one.

Key components
Conversation analytics Response quality monitoring Performance monitoring (uptime, latency) In-conversation feedback (thumbs) Reporting dashboards Continuous improvement loop
What likely exists today

Basic web analytics on government sites at best. No conversation analytics infrastructure.

Gap: Build analytics in from day one (not an afterthought) · Define KPIs before launch · Weekly failed-query review · Monthly content update cycle · Use conversation data to inform broader service design
👥
Team shape required
▶

This is not a "build it and walk away" product. Ongoing prompt engineering and content curation are permanent operational costs, not one-off project costs.

Product manager
Owns roadmap, phases, and prioritisation. Single accountable person for the platform.
Content designers (×2)
Write and maintain plain-language service content. Manage editorial standards and quality assurance.
AI/ML engineer
RAG architecture, vector DB, embeddings, prompt engineering, guardrails. Or a managed service relationship.
Integration developer
API gateway, adapters for back-end systems, data exchange standards. Grows as Phase 4 approaches.
User researcher
Channel preference research, usability testing, feedback loop from citizens back to product and content teams.
Register custodians (distributed)
Named individuals in each ministry/department responsible for keeping their register accurate and current.
Ministry content owners (distributed)
Embedded in each department. Own, update, and quality-assure service content for their area.
