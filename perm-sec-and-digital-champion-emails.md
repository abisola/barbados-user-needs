# Emails to Permanent Secretaries and Digital Champions

Draft email pairs for each thematic area surfaced in the January 2026 citizen research (2,054 respondents, 25 user needs). Each theme has:

- An email to the Permanent Secretary of the responsible Ministry / MDA
- An email to the Digital Champion working with the team on the ground

Tone is informational and collaborative – the goal is to bring perm secs into the picture and brief the Digital Champions enough that they can run with the work.

## 1. Healthcare

**Lead MDA:** Ministry of Health and Wellness (with QEH, polyclinics, and BDS)

### 1a. Email to the Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Health and Wellness

**Subject:** Citizen-led healthcare priorities for the next phase of GovTech delivery

Dear [Permanent Secretary's name],

I hope this finds you well. I'm writing to share where citizen research has pointed us in healthcare, and what we'd like to take forward with your Digital Champion in the coming weeks.

In January we ran a mixed-methods study – a national survey, a Typeform diary, and follow-up interviews – reaching 2,054 Barbadians. We asked people what causes them the most friction when they deal with government, and what they would change first. The findings have been clustered into eight thematic areas. You will see clear overlap with several of the 12 priorities of Mission Barbados, particularly those around health system reform and citizen-centred service delivery.

**Top-line summary for healthcare**

Citizens are not asking for new services – they are asking for the existing ones to be predictable. Long, early-morning queues at polyclinics, scattered records, and the absence of any way to know whether a test result is normal without a return visit are the recurring frustrations. The strongest signal is around appointment booking, with 54+ respondents describing the 6 a.m. arrival as the only realistic strategy.

**Epics we have identified**

- **H1 – Appointments without the dawn queue.** Letting people book a slot rather than physically holding a place in line.
- **H2 – Faster repeat prescriptions.** Cutting the time spent queueing for medication that has already been prescribed.
- **H3 – Health records that travel with the patient.** Records available wherever someone is treated, not siloed by clinic.
- **H4 – Simple results notifications.** Letting patients know when results are normal, without an in-person visit.

We have reached out to your Digital Champion and will be working with them to take these forward, alongside the cross-cutting challenges that show up in healthcare too – things like document checklists, status visibility, and getting through on the phone or by email.

Happy to walk you through the underlying research at any point. I'll keep you posted as we move from epics into delivery.

Kind regards,

## Cross-cutting addendum

The cross-cutting needs (X1–X8) are not "owned" by a single MDA but show up everywhere. The intent is to work them through with the Digital Champions collectively, rather than re-solve them in each ministry. The eight cross-cutting needs are:

- **X1 – Know what documents I need before I go.** Strong, 91% said important.
- **X2 – Get everything done in one visit.** Strong, 87% said important.
- **X3 – Finish the whole thing online.** Strong, 69% said important.
- **X4 – Someone actually answers when I call or email.** Strong, 50+ respondents.
- **X5 – See where my application stands without visiting.** Moderate, 56% said important.
- **X6 – Departments share my info so I'm not carrying papers.** Strong, 24+ respondents.
- **X7 – Government websites that work properly on my phone.** Moderate, 22 SPSS plus context.
- **X8 – Be told when deadlines are coming, not after I owe money.** Moderate, 27+ respondents.

Each Digital Champion email above flags the cross-cutting needs that touch their theme most directly. The aim is to combine these into a small number of reusable patterns – a checklist component, a status component, a notifications component, a contact-us component, and a mobile baseline – that we can ship once and adopt across services.
