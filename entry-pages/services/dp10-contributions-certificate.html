Get a DP-10 contributions certificate

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

The DP-10 is a National Insurance Contributions Certificate. Employers issue one to every employee at the end of each tax year. It shows the contributions that have been paid for the employee during that year.

You use the DP-10 mainly for your annual tax return – it is the document the Barbados Revenue Authority looks at to confirm your NIS contributions for the year.

If you have lost your DP-10, your first step is to ask your employer for a replacement. They are required to issue one for each tax year you worked there.

If you cannot get it from your employer – for example, because the company has closed – the National Insurance Office can usually issue a replacement directly.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the employer’s registration number and business name
  • the employee’s National Insurance Number
  • the tax year the certificate covers

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Your last payslip If you have one.

Cost

There is no fee.

How long it takes

Replacements are usually ready within 10 working days.

How to apply

Through your employer

Ask your employer for a DP-10. Employers must issue one for each employee every tax year.

Directly from the NIS

If you cannot get a copy from your employer, ask the National Insurance Office for a replacement.
