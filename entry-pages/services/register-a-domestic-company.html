Register a domestic company

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

If you want to set up a company that operates in Barbados, you must register it with the Corporate Affairs and Intellectual Property Office (CAIPO). This is the standard route for any business that is not an International Business Company or a non-profit.

Registration is done under the Companies Act, Cap. 308. A Barbadian attorney-at-law has to sign the declaration form on your behalf – the law requires it. Most people work with an attorney-at-law throughout the process, but you can also use a registered corporate services provider.

Before you start, decide on a name. Reserve the name with CAIPO first, because if it is already in use, you will need to choose another. You will also need to decide on the directors, the shareholders and the share structure.

Once incorporated, the company gets a certificate of incorporation and a unique company number. You can then open a bank account in the company’s name, register for taxes and start trading.

Who can apply

  • anyone over 18 who wants to set up a company in Barbados
  • a Barbadian attorney-at-law acting on behalf of the applicant

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the name of the company (and one or two alternatives in case it is taken)
  • the type of business the company will do
  • the address of the registered office
  • the names, addresses and dates of birth of every director
  • the names and addresses of every shareholder
  • the share structure of the company

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Articles of incorporation Signed by your attorney-at-law.
  • Notice of address of registered office (Form 4)
  • Notice of directors (Form 9)
  • Declaration form signed by your attorney-at-law
  • Photo ID for every director
  • Name reservation slip Get this first from CAIPO.
  • Proof of payment of the registration fee

Cost

Name reservation$25 BBD
Incorporation$750 BBD

How long it takes

A decision usually takes 5 to 10 working days.

How to apply

Through an attorney-at-law

A Barbadian attorney-at-law will prepare the documents and file them with the Corporate Affairs and Intellectual Property Office.
