Apply for a vanity plate

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

A vanity plate is a personalised number plate. Instead of a regular number, you choose the letters and numbers you want – for example, your initials or a meaningful word.

An Advisory Committee at the Barbados Licensing Authority reviews every application. They make sure the plate is not offensive, contentious, sensitive, sexual or in breach of a trademark. They reserve the right to accept or reject any request.

The first-time registration is $3,000 BBD. After that, you pay $1,000 BBD each year to keep the plate. These fees are clearly stated on the form itself.

If the plate you want is already in use, or the Committee rejects it, you can amend your request and apply again.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your full name, address, phone and email
  • your current registration number, if you have one
  • the wording or numbers you want on the vanity plate

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Proof of payment for the registration fee

Cost

First-time registration$3,000 BBD
Annual renewal$1,000 BBD

How long it takes

The Advisory Committee usually decides within 4 to 6 weeks.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed Application for an Approved Vanity Plate form to the Barbados Licensing Authority.

If your application is rejected

The Advisory Committee will give a reason. You can amend your request and apply again.
