Get a Contributions Payment Certificate (self-employed)

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

If you are self-employed in Barbados, you pay your own National Insurance contributions. Sometimes you need formal proof that you have done so – for example, when applying for a loan, a visa or filing your tax return.

The Contributions Payment Certificate is that proof. It is an official statement from the National Insurance Office showing what you have paid, and for which periods.

On the form, you tell the Office your details, the period the certificate should cover and what you need it for. The wording on the certificate is sometimes tailored to the purpose, so it helps to be specific.

Bring any past payment receipts you have. They make it easier for the Office to match your records and avoid delays.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your National Insurance Number and National Registration Number
  • your full name and address
  • the period the certificate should cover
  • what you need it for (so the right wording is used)

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Past NIS payment receipts If you have them, to help us match your records.

Cost

There is no fee.

How long it takes

Certificates are usually ready within 10 working days.

How to apply

In person or by post

Take or send the completed Contributions Payment Certificate form to the National Insurance Office.
