Change the engine in your vehicle

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

Every vehicle has a unique engine number. The Barbados Licensing Authority records that number when you register the vehicle. If the engine is replaced, the number changes – and the record needs to be updated to match.

You should tell the Licensing Authority within 30 days of changing the engine. You will need to say where the new engine came from – for example, from a dealer or a scrapped vehicle – and what happened to the old one.

The old engine can be sold, transferred to someone else, disposed of, or kept in your possession. The form asks you to say which.

The change is recorded against your existing registration. You should not need a new registration card unless other details have also changed.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your full name, address, phone number and date of the change
  • the registration number, chassis number and old engine number
  • the new engine number
  • where you got the new engine from
  • what happened to the old engine (sold, transferred, disposed of, or kept by you)

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Receipt or proof of where the new engine came from
  • Registration card or disc

Cost

There is no fee.

How long it takes

Approval is given the same day.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed Change of Engine form to the Barbados Licensing Authority.
