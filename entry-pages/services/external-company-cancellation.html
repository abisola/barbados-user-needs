Cancel an external company

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

An external company is a company registered overseas that has been registered to do business in Barbados. If the company stops doing business in Barbados, the law requires it to file a notice of cancellation so the external company register stays accurate.

The relevant section of the Companies Act is Section 338. The notice removes the company from the register of external companies but does not affect its existence in its home country.

Before filing the notice, the directors usually pass a resolution approving the cancellation and prepare a statement of accounts covering activities up to the cancellation date.

Most external companies file the notice through a Barbadian attorney-at-law who has been acting as their local agent.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the name and company number
  • the address of the registered office in Barbados
  • the names and addresses of the directors
  • the date the company stopped doing business in Barbados
  • the reason for cancellation

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Resolution of directors approving cancellation
  • Statement of accounts up to the cancellation date
  • Photo ID for the signing officer
  • Proof of payment of the filing fee

Cost

Filing fees are set by CAIPO. Confirm before you submit.

How long it takes

A decision usually takes 4 to 6 weeks.

How to apply

Through an attorney-at-law

A Barbadian attorney-at-law will file the notice with CAIPO.
