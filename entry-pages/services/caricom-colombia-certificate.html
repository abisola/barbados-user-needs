Apply for a CARICOM–Colombia certificate of origin

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

Barbados and CARICOM have a trade agreement with Colombia. The agreement gives qualifying Barbadian goods preferential customs treatment when they arrive in Colombia, instead of full duty.

The Certificate of Origin under this agreement is the proof that your goods qualify. It is issued by BIDC after you complete the form and provide supporting documents.

It is similar to the standard CARICOM certificate, but the rules of origin and the form layout are specific to the Colombia agreement. You will need to identify which rule of origin your goods meet.

If you regularly export to Colombia, talk to BIDC about getting set up with a streamlined process – it can save a lot of time on repeat shipments.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the exporter’s name and address
  • the consignee’s name and address in Colombia
  • the country of origin, country of destination and country of issue
  • transport information (vessel, aircraft, place of loading)
  • your exporter reference number
  • details of the goods, HS tariff number, value and gross weight

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Commercial invoice
  • Bill of lading or airway bill
  • Packing list
  • Proof that the goods meet the CARICOM–Colombia rules of origin

Cost

Confirm fees with BIDC.

How long it takes

Certificates are usually issued within 3 to 5 working days.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed CARICOM–Colombia certificate of origin to BIDC at Pelican House.
