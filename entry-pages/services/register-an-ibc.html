Register an International Business Company

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

An International Business Company (IBC) is a Barbadian company that does its business outside Barbados. Common uses include holding intellectual property, providing professional services to overseas clients, and managing offshore investments.

To register an IBC, you need both a declaration from a Barbadian attorney-at-law (the standard company declaration form) and a Government Form 1 application. The combination is what makes it an IBC rather than a regular domestic company.

An IBC must keep its records in Barbados, hold board meetings here and have a local registered office. These conditions are what allow the company to benefit from Barbados’s tax treaties and IBC tax regime.

The application is reviewed by the International Business Unit alongside CAIPO. Police record certificates are required for every director and shareholder. The whole process typically takes longer than a standard domestic company because of the extra scrutiny.

Who can apply

  • anyone who wants to set up an International Business Company in Barbados
  • a Barbadian attorney-at-law acting on behalf of the applicant

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the proposed name of the company (and alternatives)
  • the type of international business it will do
  • the name and address of the proposed registered office in Barbados
  • the names, addresses and nationalities of every director
  • the names and addresses of every shareholder
  • the share structure

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Form 1 Application for a Licence as an International Business Company
  • Articles of incorporation
  • Declaration form signed by your attorney-at-law
  • Police record certificates for every director and shareholder
  • Business plan A short summary of the planned activities.
  • Photo ID for every director and shareholder
  • Proof of payment of the application fee

Cost

Incorporation$750 BBD
Annual IBC licence$1,000 BBD

How long it takes

A decision usually takes 4 to 8 weeks.

How to apply

Through an attorney-at-law

A Barbadian attorney-at-law will file the documents with the International Business Unit and the Corporate Affairs and Intellectual Property Office.
