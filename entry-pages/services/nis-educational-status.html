Confirm a student’s educational status for NIS

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

If a child or young adult is receiving a Survivors’ Benefit or a Death Benefit and they are still in full-time education between the ages of 16 and 25, the benefit can continue. The National Insurance Office needs proof of their student status to keep paying.

The Educational Status Form is that proof. It is completed and stamped by the school, college or university, then sent to the National Insurance Office.

You will normally need a new form every academic year. The Office will pause payments if a form has not come in.

If the student takes a break from study, finishes their course, or starts working full-time, the benefit ends. Tell the National Insurance Office promptly.

Before you start

You will need:

  • the student’s name, NIS number and date of birth
  • the name and address of the school, college or university
  • the course the student is on and when it ends

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • A signed and stamped letter from the school Confirming the student is enrolled in full-time education.
  • National ID for the parent or guardian

Cost

There is no fee.

How long it takes

Confirmation is recorded within 2 weeks. You should send a new form for each new academic year.

How to apply

In person or by post

Take or send the completed Educational Status Form to the National Insurance Office.

What we've assumed on this page

This entry page is a working draft. The points below were inferred from the form or from how similar Barbadian services usually work – they have not been confirmed by the responsible agency. Please check them before this page goes live.

  • ‘Aged 16 to 25’ is the typical range for student survivors’ benefit eligibility. The form does not state the age range.
  • ‘Confirmation is recorded within 2 weeks’ is an estimate.
  • ‘New form for each new academic year’ is general practice, not explicit on the form.
