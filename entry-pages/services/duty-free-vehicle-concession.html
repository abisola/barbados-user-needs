Apply for a duty-free vehicle concession

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

Some people and businesses can bring in a vehicle without paying customs duty. This is called a duty-free concession. Returning nationals, people with disabilities, government employees, diplomats and certain authorised businesses are common categories.

There are two versions of the application: one for individuals and one for businesses. Both ask for the same vehicle details – registration, engine and chassis numbers, year and vehicle type.

Before you apply to the Barbados Licensing Authority, you usually need a letter of approval from the relevant ministry or agency. That letter is what tells the Licensing Authority you are eligible for a concession.

Once your concession is processed, the Licensing Authority will phone or email you to collect the document. The duty-free status only removes customs duty – other fees, like the licence fee, may still apply.

Who can apply

  • an individual who meets the conditions for a duty-free concession (for example, returning national, government employee, person with a disability)
  • a business authorised by the Government to receive a duty-free concession

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your name and ID number (or your business name)
  • your address, phone number and email
  • the registration number, engine number and chassis number of the vehicle
  • the year and vehicle type

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Approval letter from the relevant ministry or authority
  • Bill of sale for the vehicle
  • Business stamp Businesses only.

Cost

There is no application fee. Duty-free status removes customs duties; other fees may still apply.

How long it takes

You will receive a phone call or email when your document is ready to collect.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed Request for Duty Free Concession letter (individual or business version) to the Barbados Licensing Authority.
