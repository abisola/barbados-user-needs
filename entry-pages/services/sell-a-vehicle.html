Tell us you have sold a vehicle

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

Under Section 18 of the Road Traffic Act, if you sell a vehicle for money you must tell the Barbados Licensing Authority. This keeps the Licensing Authority’s record of who owns what up to date.

There is one version of the Sale of Vehicle form for a single owner and one version for joint owners. They ask for the same things – who you are, what the vehicle is, who you sold it to and whether you want to keep your registration number.

You can choose to keep your registration number if you want to put it on a different vehicle later. If you do not say anything, the registration number goes back into the Licensing Authority’s pool.

Bring the registration card or disc with you. The buyer will not be able to register the vehicle in their name until you have completed this step.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your full name, address, ID number, telephone number and email
  • if there is more than one owner, the names and details of every owner
  • the registration number, engine number and chassis number of the vehicle
  • the make and model of the vehicle
  • the full name and address of the person you sold it to
  • whether you want to keep your registration number

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Registration card or disc From the vehicle you have sold.
  • Receipt of sale or bill of sale
  • Photo ID for each owner

Cost

There is no fee for telling the Licensing Authority you have sold a vehicle.

How long it takes

The change is recorded the same day.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed Sale of Vehicle form (single owner or joint owner version) to the Barbados Licensing Authority.
