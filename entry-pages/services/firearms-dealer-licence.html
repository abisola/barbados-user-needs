Apply for a firearms dealer, gunsmith or collector’s licence

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

If you want to sell firearms, repair them as a gunsmith, or hold a registered collection, you need a special licence under Section 6 of the Firearms Act. This is Form C.

There are two parts to the form. Part A is for individuals applying in their own name. Part B is for companies. If a company is applying, every director and partner needs to be named.

Police record certificates are required for every named applicant. You also have to show that your premises and storage are approved by The Royal Barbados Police Force.

This is the most rigorous of the firearms licences. Decisions usually take longer than for a single-firearm licence because the Police have to check every person and every storage location.

Who can apply

  • an individual over 21 who wants to be a registered dealer, gunsmith or collector
  • a company that wants to deal in firearms

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your full name (or the company’s name)
  • your business address and storage location
  • the type of activity (dealer, gunsmith or collector)
  • the names of every director or partner of the company
  • the address of every place you will store firearms

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Two passport-size photographs of every applicant
  • Photo ID for every applicant
  • Police record certificate for every applicant
  • Certificate of incorporation For company applications.
  • Proof of approved storage
  • Proof of payment of the application fee

Cost

Fees vary by category. Pay at the Barbados Revenue Authority.

How long it takes

A decision usually takes 10 to 14 weeks.

How to apply

In person

Hand the completed Form C Application for a Firearms Dealer, Gunsmith or Collector’s Licence in to Police Headquarters, Roebuck Street, St. Michael.

Contact
