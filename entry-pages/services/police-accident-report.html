Get a police accident report

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

If you were involved in a road traffic accident in Barbados, you can ask for a copy of the police report. Insurance companies almost always need one before they will settle a claim.

The form asks for the date, time and place of the accident, the registration numbers of the vehicles involved, and what you need the report for. Anyone with a legitimate interest – the driver, the owner, the named insured, an insurance company or an attorney – can apply.

The fee is $50 BBD. Pay it at the Barbados Revenue Authority and bring the receipt with you when you submit the form.

Reports can take a few weeks to prepare, especially for serious accidents that are still being investigated. Plan around that timeline if you have a deadline from your insurer.

Who can apply

  • the driver, owner or named insured of a vehicle involved in the accident
  • an insurance company or attorney acting for someone involved

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your name, address, phone number and email
  • the date and time of the accident
  • the place of the accident
  • the registration numbers of the vehicles involved
  • your reason for asking for the report

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Proof of relationship For example, the registration card if you are the owner, or a letter of authority if you are acting for someone else.
  • Receipt of payment of the fee

Cost

Police accident report fee$50 BBD

Pay at the Barbados Revenue Authority before you apply.

How long it takes

Reports are usually ready within 4 to 6 weeks.

How to apply

In person

Hand the completed Application for Police Accident Report form in to Police Headquarters, James Street, St. Michael.

Contact
