Scrap or sell a vehicle in parts

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

When a vehicle is no longer roadworthy, you can either scrap it or sell it for parts. Either way, the Barbados Licensing Authority needs to know so the vehicle can be permanently taken off the road.

Scrapping means the vehicle as a whole is destroyed. Selling in parts means the vehicle is broken up and the parts are sold to other owners or workshops. The form lets you tick whichever applies.

Once the Licensing Authority records the change, you will no longer be liable for vehicle licence fees. The registration number is freed up and the chassis is taken off the active register.

Make sure you have the registration card or disc with you. If you also want to keep the registration number for a future vehicle, fill in the Retain Registration Number letter at the same time.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • your full name, address, phone number and email
  • a short description of the vehicle
  • the chassis number, engine number and registration number
  • whether the vehicle will be scrapped or sold in parts

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Photo ID
  • Registration card or disc

Cost

There is no fee.

How long it takes

Confirmation is usually given within 5 working days.

How to apply

In person or by post

Take the completed Scrapping of Vehicle / Selling Vehicle in Parts letter to the Barbados Licensing Authority, or send it to The Pine, East–West Boulevard, St. Michael.
