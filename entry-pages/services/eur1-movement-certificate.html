Apply for an EUR.1 movement certificate

Last updated: 12 May 2026

Overview

An EUR.1 movement certificate proves that goods you are exporting to the European Union (or another country with a similar trade agreement) qualify for preferential customs treatment. Without it, your goods face full duty on arrival.

Customs and Excise issues EUR.1 certificates in Barbados. You complete the form for each shipment and provide documents that show the goods meet the rules of origin under the relevant trade agreement.

The certificate covers a specific shipment. The next shipment needs its own certificate – there is no rolling version of EUR.1 for multiple shipments.

Some exporters can become ‘approved exporters’, which lets them self-certify rather than apply for a new EUR.1 every time. That is a separate process – ask Customs and Excise about it if you export frequently.

Before you start

You will need to know:

  • the exporter’s and consignee’s name, full address and country
  • the country, group of countries or territory of origin
  • the country, group of countries or territory of destination
  • transport details and the route
  • marks and numbers, description of the goods, packages and HS tariff code
  • gross weight or other quantity
  • invoice number and the basis for claiming origin

Documents you need

Bring with you:

  • Commercial invoice
  • Bill of lading or airway bill
  • Packing list
  • Supplier’s declaration or proof of origin Showing that the goods meet the rules of origin.

Cost

Confirm fees with the Customs and Excise Department.

How long it takes

Certificates are usually issued within 3 to 5 working days.

How to apply

In person

Take the completed EUR.1 movement certificate to the Customs and Excise Department in Bridgetown.
